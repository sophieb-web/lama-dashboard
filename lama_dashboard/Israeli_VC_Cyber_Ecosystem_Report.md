# Israeli VC & Cybersecurity Ecosystem
### Research Brief for Lama Partners — June 2026

---

## 1. The Macro Picture: Israeli VC Ecosystem

Israel punches far above its weight globally. With a population of ~10 million, it has produced **42 unicorns**, over **21,900 startups**, and raised **$94.2B** in cumulative VC/PE since inception (Tracxn, Nov 2025).

### 2024–2025 Headline Numbers
| Metric | 2024 | 2025 |
|---|---|---|
| Total Israeli tech fundraising | ~$10–12B | Continuing growth |
| Global rank | #3–5 | — |
| Active VC funds | 200+ | 200+ |
| Dry powder (Israeli VCs) | $922M | — |
| Israeli VC fundraising | $1.15B (decade low) | Recovering |

**Key tension:** Israeli VC fundraising hit a **decade low in 2024** ($1.15B across 21 funds, down from $2B/32 funds in 2023), yet actual investment *into* Israeli startups reached $10B+. This gap is increasingly filled by **global VCs** — Sequoia, Greylock, General Catalyst, Mayfield doubling down on Israeli deal flow.

### Structure of the Ecosystem
- **Early-stage funding surged 84%** in 2024; average seed round grew from $1.7M (2015) to $7M (2024)
- **55% early-stage, 45% growth-stage** by deal count
- **Tel Aviv** hosts 65% of all funding; other hubs: Jerusalem (enterprise/cyber), Herzliya (B2B SaaS), Haifa (deeptech/hardware), **Be'er Sheva** (emerging cyber, tied to military proximity)
- Government support via the **Israel Innovation Authority (IIA)** — angel investor clubs, Tnufa grants, 9 new innovation centers
- New trend: surge in **defense & dual-use tech funds** post-Oct 7 2023 war

## 2. Israeli Cybersecurity — The Crown Jewel

### By the Numbers
| Year | Total Raised | Rounds | Notable |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2023 | $1.89B | — | Post-war slowdown |
| 2024 | **$4.0B** | 89 rounds | 2x growth, Series C+ up 300% |
| 2025 | **$4.4B** | 130 rounds | New record; global VCs > Israeli VCs for first time |

- Cybersecurity accounted for **36% of total Israeli tech funding in 2024**, despite being only 7% of Israeli tech companies
- **16 Series C+ rounds in 2024** (up from 7 in 2023), raising $2.9B — a 300% jump
- **67% of cyber acquisitions in 2024** crossed the $100M threshold (nearly 3x 2019 levels)
- As of 2024, Israel had **450+ active cybersecurity startups**

### 2025 Sector Breakdown (YL Ventures State of Cyber Nation Report)
- **Security for AI**: 12 seed companies (up from 8 in 2024) — fastest growing
- **Vulnerability & Risk Management**: 20 companies — dominates overall funding
- **Endpoint Security**: 11 rounds in 2025 vs. just 1 in 2024 — dramatic recovery
- Cloud Security, Identity & Access Management also very active

### What's Driving It
1. **Mandatory military service** creates a talent pipeline with real-world high-stakes security experience
2. **Unit 8200 alumni network** (see Section 3)
3. **"Split-seed" strategy** emerging: founders deliberately pair an Israeli cyber-focused VC (for domain expertise) with a top global fund (for scale capital) in the same seed round
4. Israeli companies increasingly **acquiring other Israeli startups** — 5 such acquisitions in 2024, the most ever in 2025

## 3. Unit 8200 — The Talent Engine

Unit 8200 is the IDF's signals intelligence and cyber unit — often called "Israel's NSA." It is the single most important talent factory in the Israeli tech ecosystem.

### Why It Matters for Investors
- **Nearly 50%** of founders whose companies were acquired for $100M+ in the last decade served in Unit 8200
- Average acquisition value for **8200 alumni-founded startups: $317M** (median: $200M)
- 8200 alumni companies have at least **5 publicly traded in the US**, collectively worth ~$160B
- Major VCs including **Greylock and Sequoia** have explicitly hired partners and acquired companies to gain access to 8200 talent

### Famous 8200 Alumni Companies
| Company | Outcome | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Wiz** | Acquired by Google/Alphabet for **$32B** (March 2025) | Largest-ever Israeli exit |
| **Check Point** | Founded 1993, public, ~$20B market cap | Foundational Israeli cybersecurity |
| **CyberArk** | Public | Identity security leader |
| **Palo Alto Networks** | Public | Co-founded by Israeli 8200 veterans |
| **LightCyber, Talon Cybersecurity, Dig Security, Bridgecrew** | All acquired by Palo Alto | Each 8200-founded |
| **Koi Security** | Talks with Palo Alto, $400M (2026) | Founded 2024, already 500K+ endpoints |
| **Vega** | $700M valuation, $120M Series B (Accel) | Founded 2024 by 8200 + Granulate alumni |

### The 8200 Alumni Association
Led by CEO **Chen Shmilo**, the association actively supports entrepreneurship post-service — providing co-founder networks, mentorship, and an entrepreneurial on-ramp. They acknowledge the psychological shift: "The entrepreneurial rollercoaster is harder than serving in 8200."

### Founder Profile (Data-Backed)
- **>70% of CEOs** at acquired cyber companies came from technical roles pre-founding
- Average CEO age at $500M+ exit: **41 years old**; at $100M+ exit: **38 years old**
- Trend: founders entering with more mature strategies, raising more at seed with clearer go-to-market

## 4. The Tier 1 Israeli Cyber VC Funds

The four dominant early-stage cyber-focused Israeli VCs — by exits, unicorns, and deal flow access:

### Team8
- **Founded:** 2014 by Nadav Zafrir (now CEO of Check Point)
- **Model:** Unique — co-founds companies rather than just investing; in-house research identifies market opportunities, then recruits a founding team around them (the "Village" of C-suite advisors)
- **AUM:** ~$235M Fund II (raised March 2024); **Fund III ($250–300M) in progress** (as of Aug 2025)
- **Three arms:** Team8 Enterprise (cyber/AI/data), Team8 Fintech, Team8 Capital (VC arm)
- **Notable portfolio:** Claroty (ICS/OT security unicorn), others in data, AI, digital health

### YL Ventures
- **Founded:** ~2007; San Francisco + Tel Aviv
- **Focus:** Exclusively cybersecurity, seed stage
- **AUM:** $800M under management
- **Track record:** Seeded Axonius (unicorn), many others; repeatedly recognized by PitchBook and TIME
- **Annual publication:** *State of the Cyber Nation* report (10 years running) — the definitive data source on Israeli cyber VC

### Cyberstarts
- **Focus:** Seed-stage cybersecurity
- **Track record:** Highest number of $100M+ cyber exits of any fund; backed Wiz, Cyera, Island
- **Seeded 8 of Israel's 21 cyber unicorns** (alongside YL and Team8)

### Glilot Capital Partners
- **Founded:** 2011
- **AUM:** **$500M** (closed Sept 2025 across two funds)
- **Stage:** Stage-agnostic; Glilot Seed Fund + Glilot+ Fund (post-Series A)
- **Pace:** 8 investments in 2025 already; portfolio companies attracted $700M in follow-on in 2025 alone
- **Sectors:** Cybersecurity + AI/SaaS

### NightDragon (Context)
- US-based but deeply active in Israeli cyber M&A; published a comprehensive Israeli Cyber M&A report in 2024 covering 116 deals since 2019

## 7. Macro Trends to Track

1. **AI + Cyber convergence** — "Security for AI" is the fastest-growing seed category in 2025; every major fund is repositioning
2. **Defense/dual-use tech boom** — Post-Oct 7, new dedicated defense tech funds forming; government accelerating IIA support
3. **Global VCs displacing local VCs** — For the first time in 2025, global VCs (Sequoia, Greylock, General Catalyst) are the *leading* source of Israeli cyber seed capital
4. **Israeli companies acquiring Israeli companies** — Ecosystem maturity signal; scale-ups buying early-stage companies
5. **"Split-seed" rounds** — Founders deliberately pairing Israeli domain-expert VCs with global funds in the same seed round
6. **Be'er Sheva rising** — Government cyber programs + Ben-Gurion University + military proximity creating a second cyber hub
