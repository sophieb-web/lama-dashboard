## Tier 1 — Israeli Cyber-Specialist VCs
*These are the funds that define the Israeli cyber ecosystem. Having one of these as your lead is the strongest signal for a seed-stage Israeli cyber company.*

### CyberStarts
- **Type:** Israeli VC, cyber-only
- **Active portfolio (PitchBook):** 18
- **Stage:** Seed
- **Known for:** Highest number of $100M+ cyber exits of any fund; seeded Wiz, Cyera, Island
- **Key people:** Gili Raanan (founder)
- **2025 fund raise:** $680M across two funds — largest Israeli cyber VC raise ever
- **Notable investments:** Wiz (acquired Google $32B), Cyera, Island, Vega (seed), Onyx, Blockaid, Fabrix Security, Aim Security
- **Model:** Takes the seed position in companies it believes can be category-defining; very selective; known for backing teams before product

### YL Ventures
- **Type:** Israeli-American VC, cyber-only
- **Active portfolio (PitchBook):** 15
- **Stage:** Seed
- **HQ:** San Francisco + Tel Aviv
- **AUM:** $800M
- **Known for:** Publishes the annual *State of the Cyber Nation* report (the definitive industry data source); backed Axonius (unicorn), Hunters, many others; PitchBook and TIME recognized as top performer
- **Key people:** Yoav Leitersdorf (Managing Partner), Or Salom (analyst/report author)
- **Model:** Exclusively cyber, exclusively Israeli founders; strong CISO network for customer intro support

### Team8
- **Type:** Israeli VC + company builder, cyber/AI/data
- **Active portfolio (PitchBook):** 50
- **Stage:** Seed through Series B
- **HQ:** Tel Aviv
- **AUM:** ~$235M Fund II (raised Mar 2024); Fund III ($250–300M) in progress as of Aug 2025
- **Key people:** Nadav Zafrir (founder, now CEO of Check Point); Sarit Firon, Liran Grinberg, Hadar Siterman Norris (Fund partners)
- **Model:** Unique — co-founds companies rather than just investing. In-house research identifies market gaps, then recruits founding teams. "The Village" of C-suite advisors supports portfolio companies.
- **Three arms:** Team8 Enterprise (cyber/AI/data), Team8 Fintech, Team8 Capital (VC arm)
- **Notable portfolio:** Claroty (OT/ICS security unicorn), Torq (led Series D)

### Glilot Capital Partners
- **Type:** Israeli VC, cyber + SaaS
- **Active portfolio (PitchBook):** 35
- **Stage:** Seed + growth (two funds: Glilot Seed Fund, Glilot+ post-Series A)
- **AUM:** $500M (closed Sept 2025 across two funds)
- **Known for:** Top 5 best-performing VC funds globally (various rankings); 22 exits; portfolio attracted $700M follow-on in 2025 alone; 8 new investments in 2025
- **Model:** Stage-agnostic; invests from seed through growth; cybersecurity and enterprise SaaS

### Merlin Ventures
- **Type:** US-based, Israeli-focused, cyber-only
- **Active portfolio (PitchBook):** 15
- **Stage:** Seed (fund positioning) + growth
- **AUM:** $75M Fund II (raised Jun 2025, oversubscribed); prior $53M Fund I
- **Key people:** Shay Michel (Managing Partner), Seth Spergel (Managing Partner), David Phelps (CEO Merlin Group)
- **Fund I track record:** 10 investments, 6 acquired — including Talon Security and Dig Security (both Palo Alto Networks), InfoSec Global (Keyfactor), Veriti (Check Point)
- **Notable:** Led Torq's $140M Series D (Jan 2026); advisory board includes Torq's Ofer Smadari, Talon's Ofer Ben-Noon, Dig's Dan Benjamin
- **Differentiation:** US-based with deep Israel roots; bridge fund helping Israeli cyber companies penetrate US federal/government markets

## Key Corporate/Accelerator Programs Most Active in Israeli Cyber

### AWS & CrowdStrike Cybersecurity Accelerator
- **Active portfolio (PitchBook):** 52 — **most active single "investor" in the screener**
- **What it is:** Joint accelerator run by Amazon Web Services and CrowdStrike; provides cloud credits, go-to-market support, and introductions to enterprise customers
- **Significance:** Appears in ~10 companies in the tracker; being accepted is a credibility signal and provides real commercial traction via AWS and CrowdStrike customer networks
- **Examples from tracker:** Saiflow, Twine Security, Torq, Pluto Security, Pillar Security, Remedio, Opti, and others

### Intel Ignite
- **What it is:** Intel's startup accelerator; no equity; provides mentorship, engineering support, and Intel customer network
- **Appears in:** Tonic Security, Oligo Security, Zenity, Copperhelm, Legion, Ox Security
- **Significance:** Strong signal for deep tech / hardware-adjacent cyber companies

### Mamram Space
- **What it is:** Israeli military computing unit (MAMRAM) alumni-run accelerator; overlaps with 8200 alumni network
- **Appears in:** SimplicoAI, Port0, Plasma Security

- **Lama is not in the top 35** of this screener by active portfolio size — which is expected given fund size; they are pre-seed/seed and portfolio is concentrated
- **Lama's natural co-investors** based on deal history: Firestreak Ventures, Alumni Ventures, IBM Ventures

## Summary: The Operator-Angel Network

A distinctive feature of Israeli cyber is the density of operator-angels — founders of major Israeli cyber companies who personally reinvest into the next generation. From the full PitchBook screener, the confirmed operator-angels are:

| Angel | Day Job | Active Portfolio |
|---|---|---|
| Assaf Rappaport | CEO, Wiz (post-Google acquisition) | 17 |
| Yevgeny Dibrov | CEO, Armis (post-ServiceNow acquisition) | 7 |
| Yotam Segev | CEO, Cyera (unicorn) | 5 |
| Ofir Ehrlich | CEO, Eon | 9 |
| George Kurtz | CEO, CrowdStrike | 13 |
| Ofer Smadari | CEO, Torq | 3 |
| Amir Jerbi | Co-founder, Aqua Security | 29 |
| Adi Sharabani | Serial founder (Skycure → Symantec) | 11 |
| Amiram Shachar | Co-founder, Noname Security + Upwind | 3 |
| Ariel Maislos | Serial founder (Anobit → Apple) | 20 |

This network is one of the most powerful dynamics in Israeli cyber — founders who've had major exits immediately reinvest, providing both capital and credibility to the next wave of companies.
