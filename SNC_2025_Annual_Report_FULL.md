## Executive Summary — The Big Picture

2025 was a **definitive turning point** for Israeli tech. Two forces drove everything:
1. **AI** changed how value is created *inside* companies — productivity per employee up, development cycles compressed
2. **Global & regional shifts** (Abraham Accords, US strategic demand) determined where that value is scaled and realized

The ecosystem is transitioning from "Startup Nation" → **"Scale-Up Nation"**: fewer but larger companies, leaner workforces, higher productivity per employee, and more capital concentrated in proven winners.

## Private Funding Breakdown by Stage

| Stage | 2025 Capital | vs 2024 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Seed** | $1.4B | +48% | Fastest growing stage |
| **Series A** | $2.8B | +6% | 129 rounds; median $16M (up 25%) |

| **Late stage (C+)** | $2.5B | -24% | 17 rounds; investor caution at later stages |

**Key insight:** Series B is the engine of 2025 — the "scale-up transition" stage. Series A to B now takes a median of **27 months** (all-time high). **Cybersecurity fastest to first round:** 10-month median from founding to first round vs. 15-month ecosystem average.

### Israeli Companies as Acquirers
In 2025, Israeli companies acted as acquirers in **38% of all acquisitions by volume** — but only 2% of total value. Key Israeli acquirers:
- **Check Point** — multiple smaller acquisitions
- **Cato Networks** — acquired Aim Security ($350M, AI security)
- **Cyera** — acquired Otterize
- **Pentera** — acquired DevOcean
- **Axonius, Armis, Orca Security, Upwind** — all made Israeli startup acquisitions

Pattern: Large Israeli companies raised growth capital specifically to acquire smaller Israeli startups, compressing development timelines and expanding platforms faster than organic growth alone.

## AI in the Israeli Ecosystem

### Classification Framework
SNC uses a 5-tier AI classification (from PitchBook):
1. **Vertical Applications, AI-First** — AI is core engine of industry-specific product (~50% of Israeli AI companies)
2. **Vertical Applications, Support** — AI augments existing workflows (~27%)
3. **Horizontal Platforms** — cross-industry AI infrastructure (~15%)
4. **Autonomous Machines** — robotics/autonomous tech (~6%)
5. **Semiconductors** — compute/hardware (~3%)

### Where the Money Goes
- **Vertical Applications** = 76% of company count, but only 27% of funding value
- **Horizontal Platforms** = 15% of companies, but **56% of funding value** and 52% of M&A value
- Pattern: Many companies building vertical AI apps, but money concentrates in infrastructure/platform plays

### AI Funding Growth in 2025
- Reported AI capital up **78%** even as AI deal count fell 11% — bigger checks into fewer AI companies
- Horizontal Platforms: events +6%, funding +79%
- Vertical AI-First: deal count -14%, funding nearly doubled

## Economic Impact

- High-tech exports = **56% of Israel's total exports** (up from 52% in 2023)
- High-tech employment: ~619K globally (peaked at 623K mid-2025, slight pullback H2)
- Tech workforce = **16.3–16.4%** of total Israeli workforce
- R&D = ~50% of high-tech workforce; growing roles in Product/QA/Data (+11%)
- Software developer roles declining — AI-assisted coding reducing demand for generalist engineers
- Productivity per employee surging — more output with same or fewer people

---

## Regional: Abraham Accords & MENA

- **UAE** is the primary investment corridor — most active, most consistent
- MENA investments concentrated in: Agri/Food Tech (21% of rounds), Cybersecurity, Aerospace/Defense, Industrial Tech
- Pattern: early-stage, small checks, exploratory — building relationships not deploying scale capital yet
- Defense tech emerging as the next major category: "Its scale is approaching what cyber became in previous years" — expected $100B in Israeli defense exits over next 5 years, with demand from Japan, Germany, Saudi Arabia, UAE

## Key Structural Takeaways for Lama Context

1. **Scale-Up Nation shift is real** — ecosystem maturing from startup formation to scaling and exits
2. **Cybersecurity is structurally dominant** — highest funding per deal, fastest to raise, best financial stability, most M&A activity as % of sector
3. **Series B is the hot stage** — +84% capital growth; companies graduating from seed/A in bigger numbers
4. **AI is horizontal, not vertical, in value** — platforms capture 56% of capital despite 15% of companies; vertical AI apps are many but small
5. **Israeli companies are now acquirers** — ecosystem maturity signal; Cato, Cyera, Pentera, Axonius all buying Israeli startups
6. **Defense tech is next** — approaching cyber-level opportunity, especially with Gulf/MENA demand
7. **Global VCs moving to seed** — for the first time outpacing Israeli VCs at seed stage
8. **Fewer investors, bigger checks** — total investor count down 17% (Israeli) and 31% (global), but remaining investors deploying more
